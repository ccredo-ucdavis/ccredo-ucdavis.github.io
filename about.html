About Me

Hello! My name is Collin Credo, and I am an Aerospace Engineering student at the University of California, Davis with interests in controls engineering, automation, HVAC systems, energy efficiency, and aerospace technologies. I currently work as an Energy Engineering Intern with UC Davis Facilities Management, where I collaborate with controls engineers to analyze building performance, diagnose HVAC system issues, and develop solutions that improve energy efficiency across campus facilities.

My technical experience includes building automation systems, HVAC diagnostics, MATLAB application development, experimental data analysis, and engineering problem-solving. Prior to transferring to UC Davis, I earned two Associate Degrees from San Joaquin Delta College, which has allowed me to accelerate my academic progress with the goal of graduating a year early and pursuing graduate studies.

I am particularly interested in the intersection of energy efficiency and emerging technologies. As industries such as artificial intelligence continue to drive demand for large-scale computing infrastructure, I believe efficient thermal management and cooling systems will play an increasingly important role in the future of engineering. In addition to my work in energy engineering, I am involved with the UC Davis chapter of the Vertical Flight Society, where I am contributing to a project focused on hybridizing a tiltrotor aircraft.

Beyond Engineering

Outside of engineering, I enjoy playing on the worship team for my Christian Fellowship: Catalyst, playing the clarinet and bass clarinet for the Video Game Orchestra and UC Davis Concert Band, working on automotive projects like gas efficiency mods, exploring and experimenting with different technologies such as mini breadboard projects and writing scripts/plays for UC Davis Philippine Cultural Night.
